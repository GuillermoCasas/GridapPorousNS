| 2026-05-21 | Feeding the τ velocity-floor (`effective_speed`) into the physical Forchheimer reaction speed | `effective_speed` carries a mesh-dependent diffusive floor `u_base + h_floor_weight·c₁ν/(c₂h)` that scales like ν/h and **grows** under refinement. That is acceptable inside τ (a stabilization parameter that multiplies a vanishing strong residual) but, fed into the drag `σ=a(α)+b(α)\|u\|`, it makes each mesh solve a different effective drag law. For the Cocquet benchmark (Re=500, Forchheimer, h_floor_weight=0.1) the floor reached 0.128 at the N=80 reference — larger than \|u\|_max≈0.125 — collapsing velocity L² convergence to ~0.14–0.53 (paper and MMS both give ≥2). Invisible to: pure NS (σ=0), constant-σ MMS (speed unused), and any Forchheimer MMS run with `h_floor_weight=0` (where `reaction_speed≡effective_speed`). | Introduced `reaction_speed` (constant `u_base_floor_ref` floor only, mesh-independent) for the physical reaction; `SigOp`/`DSigOp` use it while τ keeps `effective_speed`. Cocquet config sets `h_floor_weight=0`. Verified: Cocquet velocity H¹ slopes 1.94/2.03 (optimal), L² 2.80/2.32 (was 0.14/0.53); constant-σ MMS unchanged. Diagnosed via a new manufactured solution with the *identical* Forchheimer+SymmetricGradient operator (`test/extended/CocquetFormMMS`), which proved the formulation itself was optimal and the source paper reports O(h²) at the same parameters. | `regularization.jl`, `continuous_problem.jl` (`SigOp`/`DSigOp`), `mms_paper_2d.jl`, `CocquetExperiment/data/test_config.json` | `reaction`, `Forchheimer`, `regularization`, `MMS`, `verification` |
| 2026-05-26 | Attempted "fix" for sub-optimal slope at extreme-parameter MMS cells by reversing the eps_pert iteration order (hard → easy → easy → hard) | **WITHDRAWN by author 2026-05-26.** Diagnostic was correct: a velocity-centering probe (`diagnostics/velocity_centering_probe.jl`) showed config_18 (Re=Da=10⁶, α=0.5) reaches a 30× tighter residual and a ~2× lower L²(u) at N=160 when Newton starts from x₀=u_ex (eps_pert=0) versus from a perturbed guess (eps_pert=1.0). But the inference — that the original hard→easy order was a bug — misread the harness's design intent.
