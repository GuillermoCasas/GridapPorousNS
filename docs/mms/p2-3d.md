## A. The P2-3D accuracy defect — "converged-but-wrong"

**Symptom.** On structured Kuhn tetrahedra, k=2 (P2) MMS converges the *nonlinear solve* but to a *wrong field*
(20–95× the interpolant error, non-monotone under refinement). **k=1 works; 2D-P2-on-triangles works** (rates
2.97/1.92) — so it is genuinely **3D-specific**, not P2-itself and not simplex-specific.

**Leading explanation — coercivity margin.** The viscous 2nd-derivative subscale is **anti-coercive by
construction**: the viscous operator is self-adjoint, so the stabilization form `B_S` carries `−τ‖𝓛_visc V‖²`.
Coercivity survives only if the Galerkin viscous form dominates it, which requires **`c₁ > 2ξ·C_inv²`**, where
`C_inv²` is an element-local generalized eigenvalue (`h_diam²·max ‖∇·εᵈv‖²/‖εᵈv‖²`). The paper's fixed
**`c₁ = 4k⁴`** has near-zero margin for high-`C_inv` 3D tets: measured **`C_inv² ≈ 214` for the Kuhn tet vs `60`
for the Q2 quad** — and this is **mesh-independent** (`λ_max ∝ 1/h²` cancels `h²`, so the violation does *not*
self-heal under refinement). Kuhn shape-regularity `h/ρ = 8.36` is the worst. So the structured Kuhn mesh sits
**over the coercivity edge** at `4k⁴`.

**Two independent proofs it is a coercivity loss, not a code bug:**
1. **Céa's lemma, from the error alone.** Best-approx ≈ 1e-3, observed error ≈ 0.049 ⇒ `M/α ≳ 50` ⇒ coercivity
   ≈ 0. A coercive form *cannot* give a 50× error — this refutes any "definite-but-buggy" reading.
2. **`c₁×4` restores a monotone convergent sweep** (no revert at the finest mesh; `c₁×2` still reverts). The
   remedy is theory-sanctioned: **article.tex line 910** explicitly prescribes element-aware `c₁` ("the optimal
   value depends on element type through `C_inv`").

### Direct coercivity measurement (2026-07-09) — inference → measurement

The two proofs above are *inference*. A direct diagnostic now **measures** the coercivity constant: assemble the
ASGS momentum tangent at the MMS interpolant (π_h = 0), take the velocity (1,1)-block symmetric part
`S = ½(Aᵤᵤ + Aᵤᵤᵀ)`, and compute the smallest generalized eigenvalue `λ_min` of `(S, G)` against the Galerkin
deviatoric-viscous + reaction energy Gram `G`. **`λ_min > 0 ⟺ coercive`.** The probe scales **c₁ alone** (c₂
frozen) — the first clean c₁-only experiment (every `c1_mult` hook in the repo co-scales c₂). Script:
`test/extended/ManufacturedSolutions3D/coercivity_probe.jl`; figure: `results/debug_results/coercivity_probe.png`.

**Results (Kuhn P2, Re = Da = 1, α₀ = 0.5):**

- **Paper `c₁ = 4k⁴` is measured sub-coercive** on every *interior* structured-Kuhn mesh:
  `λ_min(c₁×1) = −0.057 (6³), −0.15 (8³), −0.41 (12³)` — negative, and *deepening* under refinement (genuine
  interior high-frequency modes emerge; the coarse (4,4,1), boundary-dominated, stays coercive at every `c₁`).
  This **upgrades the Céa inference to a direct measurement** and closes the "definite-but-buggy" reading: the
  assembled form is genuinely non-coercive at paper `c₁`.
- **`λ_min` is a property of the bilinear form, not the solver:** Exact-Newton and Picard tangents give
  **identical `λ_min` to 4 sig figs** — corroborating the "Newton ≡ Picard, identical wrong root" observation
  (§ *Do NOT re-chase*) and exonerating the linearization.
- **The threshold is convention-invariant in `c₁/h²`** (the physical τ₁ scale), measured **≈ 90–100**; so its
  expression as a *multiple* of `4k⁴ = 64` depends entirely on which `h` is plugged into τ:

  | h-convention in τ | coercivity threshold as ×4k⁴ | `c₁` at threshold |
  |---|---|---|
  | `:shortest_edge` (h = a) | ≈ 1.4× | ≈ 90 |
  | `:regular_tet` (h ≈ 1.12a, `smoke3d.jl` sweep default) | ≈ 2.0× | ≈ 127 |
  | **`:diameter` (h ≈ 1.73a — the paper's `h_K`)** | **≈ 4×** | **≈ 254** |

**Verdict — the robust worst-case `c₁` STANDS (adopted policy).** The historical "`c₁×4` / `≈3.6×`" is *not*
overkill: it is the measured coercivity threshold **stated in the paper's own `:diameter` `h_K` convention**
(and consistent with the worst-element `c₁ ≳ 2ξ·C_inv²`, `C_inv² ≈ 214` in diameter units). Smaller multipliers
*do* reach coercivity in smaller-`h` conventions (≈ 1.4× in `:shortest_edge`), and this is documented for
completeness — but the **adopted value is the worst-case element-aware `c₁`**, a deliberately *conservative*
(robust) choice, **not** a necessary-minimum. Do **not** tune `c₁` down to the marginal threshold. (Corollary:
the "×4 vs ×1.4" spread is purely the h-convention; unifying the 3D harness `h` to a named production convention —
[pending-tasks §2b/1g] — is what makes the printed `c₁` unambiguous.)

**Reconciliation with Kratos.** At paper `c₁` the deficit is *marginal* (`|λ_min| ≈ 0.05–0.15`, a few % of the
O(1) energy norm). A small setup difference flips the sign into coercivity: a **pseudo-transient τ** (any finite
δt shrinks τ₁ exactly like raising `c₁`), a **larger `h` in the τ definition**, or a **better-conditioned tet**.
"Kratos optimal at paper `c₁` / Gridap just over the edge" is therefore **two implementations straddling a
marginal threshold**, not a contradiction — the cleanest account yet of the last open caveat. It does *not*
resurrect the withdrawn "term-level Gridap↔paper discrepancy" framing: the measurement localizes the effect to
coercivity of the assembled form, exactly as the coercivity-margin verdict predicts.

### Do NOT re-chase (refuted, with verdicts)

Viscous-adjoint **sign** (a small *positive* mult heals too), **inf-sup** (Taylor–Hood P2/P1 equally wrong),
**mesh quality per se** (Frontal unstructured also erratic), **anisotropy** (isotropic cube equally bad),
**Newton-vs-Picard** (identical wrong root ⇒ the defect is in the *assembled residual `F`*, not the
Jacobian/linearization), **simplex-vs-3D** (2D-TRI-P2 optimal), **h-convention** (a real 2D/3D inconsistency but
the margin is h-independent), grad-div / quadrature / oracle / individual code factors (exhaustive audit, none
found). The nonlinear iteration is **rigorously exonerated**: logged `‖R‖` is the raw assembled residual, the
Exact-Newton Jacobian passes a Taylor test `O(ε²)`, and bare Newton from the interpolant lands on production's
solution (velocity DOFs agree ~3e-6) — the sub-optimal field is a **genuine converged discrete fixed point**.

**Remedy & hooks.** Element-aware `c₁` (line 910), *not* a code change. Hooks: `smoke3d.jl` (`c1_mult`, `h_conv`,
ablation params), `continuous_problem.jl` (`VISC_ADJ_MULT` env), `tau.jl` (`TAU_VISC_MULT` env),
`phase1_tri_k2.json` (2D-TRI-P2 positive control). **Memory wall:** direct LU OOMs past ~30–40k DOF on 32 GB
(P2-3D is confined to a small LU window; ILU-GMRES stalls on the `(u,p)` saddle — JFNK is the path, see C).

## B. Iterative-penalty fix — *well-posedness* (SOLVED, separate from A)

3D **all-Dirichlet at `ε=0` is ill-posed** (fully-incompressible, pressure defined only up to a constant).
Codina's iterative penalty adds **`ε_num·(pⁿ − pⁿ⁻¹)`** to the **mass residual** (article.tex line 1383), lagging
`pⁿ⁻¹` on the RHS. **The bug that hid this:** `ε_num` was in the *Jacobian only*, not the residual (a comment
misread `pⁿ⁻¹` as the Newton increment `dp`), so it cancelled at convergence and did nothing. **Fix:** add the
term to the residual, wrap the solve in an outer penalty loop, stop at relative drift `< xtol`. Implementation:
`continuous_problem.jl` (`p_prev` kwarg), `solver_core.jl` (`_one_pass` outer loop), `osgs_solver.jl` (thread
`p_prev`). Flag `iterative_penalty_enabled` (default OFF). **This fixes well-posedness only — it is NOT the P2
accuracy fix** (that instinct was withdrawn).

## C. OSGS ∂π/∂u coupling — (SOLVED 2026-07-09)

The OSGS staggered/JFNK solve stalled in 3D because the frozen-π → full-tangent coupling is violently
non-contractive: **`ρ(J_frozen⁻¹·∂π/∂u) ≈ 1249` at paper `c₁`** (2D ≈ 0.88), so neither GMRES nor the
staggered-π update can descend (the solver sits at the exact-guess interpolant, `‖f‖` stuck ~1.7e-3 — this is
what earlier *looked like* a "correct root" but was an artifact). **Fix (solution-preserving): assemble the
frozen-π PRECONDITIONER at `c₁×4` while the residual + tangent stay at paper `c₁`** — `ρ_prec` drops **1249 →
3.8** (U-shaped: ×2→93, ×4→3.8, ×8→7.5). The residual is untouched, so the root is unchanged. Implementation:
`osgs_solver.jl` `jac_precond_fn` closure, config `osgs_jfnk_precond_c1_mult` (default 1.0); `smoke3d.jl` OSGS-P2
uses `mult=4` + `jfnk_maxiter=80`. **Feedback into (A):** once the coupling is fixed the true paper-`c₁` root is
reached — and it reveals genuine **pressure under-stabilization at paper `c₁`** (L²p ≈ 0.045 vs ≈ 0.003 at
`c₁×4`). A real saddle-point / MG preconditioner would be the principled fix ([pending-tasks §3d](../pending-tasks.md)).

## Supporting method notes

- **`eps_pert` homotopy (3D).** Initial-guess perturbation (hard→easy descent):
  `u0 = u_ex + eps_p·(‖u_ex‖/‖h_pert‖)·h_pert` with `h_pert` a boundary-vanishing bubble, plus **reference-root
  matching** (tol 1e-3) to reject spurious roots (spurious `O(1)`, true root agrees ~1e-6). `smoke3d.jl
  solve_one`; commit `5ecc0ca`.
- **Boot-skip is critical for OSGS-3D.** The ASGS Stage-I boot (code globalization, not in the paper algorithm)
  converges to a *different* ASGS fixed point that OSGS overshoots from. `osgs_skip_asgs_boot=true` lets OSGS
  staggering run from the guess directly (`eps_pert` supplies globalization); OSGS-P1 then converges from the
  hardest `eps_pert=1` start. **Without boot-skip the JFNK recipe hangs.**
- **3D P1-ASGS is method-intrinsically L²-sub-optimal** (L²u ≈ 1.2 ASGS vs 2.0 OSGS on the same mesh) — an
  Aubin–Nitsche / orthogonal-projection-consistency effect, *not* a bug; OSGS recovers it. ([pending-tasks §2d](../pending-tasks.md))
- **OSGS↔ASGS ordering is regime-dependent.** Low Da (convection/pressure) → OSGS projects the FE-representable
  residual → better L²; high Da (reaction-dominated) → OSGS discards reactive terms → weaker control. Same
  mechanism, opposite sign by regime.
