\section{Motivation}

The stabilized FE solver in this codebase is dimensional. Its inputs are
$(\rho, \mu, \sigma, U, L, \boldsymbol{f})$ in a chosen system of units.
The Method of Manufactured Solutions (MMS) test harness, however, organises
runs by the \emph{dimensionless} regime $(\Reyn, \Damk, \alpha_0)$, and is
free to choose any consistent dimensional encoding of each dimensionless
cell. Here $\alpha_0$ is the infimum of the porosity field $\alpha(x)$, and
$\alpha_\infty = 1$ is its supremum (a convention shared with
\texttt{article.tex}).

Until recently, the harness fixed
\begin{equation}
  U = 1, \qquad L = 1,
\end{equation}
and derived $\nu, \sigma$ from the dimensionless definitions used in
\texttt{article.tex}:
\begin{equation}
  \nu \;=\; \frac{U\,L}{\Reyn} \;=\; \frac{1}{\Reyn},
  \qquad
  \sigma \;=\; \frac{\Damk\,\alpha_\infty\,\nu}{L^2}
                          \;=\; \frac{\Damk\,\alpha_\infty}{\Reyn}.
\end{equation}
For the sweep grid $\Reyn \in \{10^{-6}, 1, 10^{6}\}$,
$\Damk \in \{10^{-6}, 1, 10^{6}\}$, $\alpha_0 \in \{1, 0.5, 0.05\}$
(with $\alpha_\infty = 1$), this encoding produces values such as
$\nu = 10^{6}$ and $\sigma = 10^{12}$ for the cell
$(\Reyn=10^{-6}, \Damk=10^{6}, \alpha_0=1)$. Twelve orders of magnitude
separate the smallest from the largest dimensional coefficient that the
solver sees, \emph{all on the same side of unity}.

While the dimensionless physics is unchanged, this one-sided spread breaks
numerical components of the stabilized solver that are not scale-invariant
once round-off enters:
\begin{itemize}
  \item the OSGS staggered fixed-point's $L^2$ projection
        $\pi_h$ of the strong residual evaluates
        $\sigma\,\boldsymbol{u}_h$ and $\nu \Delta \boldsymbol{u}_h$ at
        near-precision-floor $\boldsymbol{u}_h$ when
        $\boldsymbol{u}_h \sim 1/\sigma$, amplifying floating-point noise
        into a non-contractive $\pi_h$ update sequence;
  \item the assembled Jacobian's row magnitudes span 12 orders, which the
        direct factorisation absorbs but the Newton merit function and
        line-search tolerances do not;
  \item the MMS forcing oracle, computed by symbolic differentiation of the
        analytic exact solution, mixes $\sigma\,\boldsymbol{u}_{\mathrm{ex}}$
        at magnitude $10^{12}$ with sub-dominant terms at $10^{6}$, losing
        six digits of relative precision in the sub-dominant contributions.
\end{itemize}

\section{The centered encoding}

For a fixed dimensionless cell $(\Reyn, \Damk, \alpha_\infty)$, the
harness has \emph{two} free dimensional parameters $(U, L)$. The freedom
follows from the constraint pair (matching \texttt{article.tex} eq.~3.4):
\begin{align}
  \Reyn  &= \frac{U\,L}{\nu}, &
  \Damk  &= \frac{\sigma\,L^2}{\alpha_\infty\,\nu}.
\end{align}
Eliminating $\nu, \sigma$:
\begin{equation}
  \nu\,\sigma
   \;=\; \frac{\alpha_\infty\,\Damk\,U^{2}}{\Reyn^{2}},
  \qquad
  \frac{\nu}{\sigma}
   \;=\; \frac{L^{2}}{\alpha_\infty\,\Damk}.
\end{equation}
So $U$ controls the geometric mean and $L$ controls the spread.

\paragraph{Choice.}
The \emph{centered encoding} chooses $U$ so that
$\sqrt{\nu\,\sigma} = 1$, leaving $L = 1$ because the manufactured solution
shape $\sin(\pi x)$ is hard-coded for a unit domain (see
\texttt{Paper2DMMS} in \texttt{src/problems/mms\_paper\_2d.jl}):
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;\;
    U \;=\; \frac{\Reyn}{\sqrt{\alpha_\infty\,\Damk}},
    \qquad
    L \;=\; 1.
  \;\;}
\end{equation}
The resulting dimensional coefficients are
\begin{equation}
  \nu \;=\; \frac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha_\infty\,\Damk}},
  \qquad
  \sigma \;=\; \sqrt{\alpha_\infty\,\Damk}.
\end{equation}
One checks immediately that
$\nu\,\sigma = 1$ (centering condition) and
$\nu/\sigma = 1/(\alpha_\infty\,\Damk)$ (intrinsic spread).
Note that $\nu$ and $\sigma$ no longer depend on $\Reyn$ â€” only $U$ absorbs
the $\Reyn$ variation. The spread
$\sigma / \nu = \alpha_\infty\,\Damk$ is \emph{intrinsic} to the cell and
cannot be compressed by any choice of $(U, L)$; centering only shifts it
to be symmetric about unity.

\paragraph{What this does \emph{not} change.}
The dimensionless equation (and therefore the dimensionless solution
$\boldsymbol{u}^*, p^*$) is identical between the default and centered
encodings; this is a strict reparametrization. The discrete velocity
\emph{degrees of freedom} are
$\boldsymbol{u}_h^{\mathrm{node}} \approx U\,\boldsymbol{u}^{*,\mathrm{node}}$,
so they take on different absolute magnitudes between encodings, but their
dimensionless counterparts $\boldsymbol{u}^{*,\mathrm{node}}$ are unchanged.
The $H^1$-seminorm error
$|\boldsymbol{u}_h - \boldsymbol{u}_{\mathrm{ex}}|_{H^1(\Omega)}\,/\,(U/L)$
is regime-invariant (verified empirically: agreement to $<\!1\%$ across
encodings on the easy regime C2).

\paragraph{Maximum dimensional coefficient.}
The worst cells under the default encoding (C7, C16, C23) hit
$\sigma = 10^{12}$. Under the centered encoding the worst is
$\sigma = 10^{3}$ (any cell with $\Damk = 10^{6}$). The maximum dimensional
coefficient drops by nine orders of magnitude across the sweep.

\section{Verification evidence}

Two single-cell probes
(\texttt{test/extended/ManufacturedSolutions/diagnostics/jacobian\_equilibration\_osgs\_probe.jl})
validated the reparametrization before the full rerun. Note that the
probe used a slightly different centering formula
$U = \Reyn/\sqrt{\alpha_0 \Damk}$ (with $\alpha_0 = 0.5$ for C16), so
$U = 1.41\cdot10^{-9}$ and $\sigma = 1.41\cdot10^{3}$ in the probe;
the harness rerun uses $U = \Reyn/\sqrt{\alpha_\infty \Damk}$ with
$\alpha_\infty = 1$, giving $U = 10^{-9}$ and $\sigma = 10^{3}$ exactly.

With $\Reyn=1$, $\Damk=10^{-6}$, $\alpha_0=1$, both encodings should give
the same answer up to round-off.

\noindent The $H^{1}$ semi-norm error agrees to under $1\%$ between
encodings, confirming that the centered encoding represents the same
discrete physics. The $L^{2}$ values differ by a constant noise-floor
factor: in both runs the absolute residual at convergence is $O(10^{-9})$,
and that absolute noise is divided by $U_c = U$ when forming the
normalized error â€” a $1000\times$ different denominator produces the
apparent $5\times$ discrepancy without any discretisation disagreement.

\section{Implementation}

The implementation is a single-line change to the MMS harness; no
modification to the dimensional solver code:

\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.9\linewidth}
\begin{lstlisting}[basicstyle=\ttfamily\small, frame=single]
# test/extended/ManufacturedSolutions/run_test.jl, ~line 523

# Before (default encoding):
U_amp = 1.0
L     = 1.0

# After (centered encoding):
U_amp = Re / sqrt(alpha_infty * Da)
L     = 1.0
\end{lstlisting}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}

The remainder of the harness pipeline (\texttt{build\_mms\_formulation},
\texttt{Paper2DMMS}, \texttt{evaluate\_exactness\_diagnostics},
\texttt{get\_characteristic\_scales}) already threads $U$ and $L$ through
correctly: the MMS exact velocity is multiplied by $U$, the manufactured
forcing is computed analytically from that scaled solution, and error
norms divide out $U_c = U$ to report dimensionless quantities. No tooling
surgery is required.

\section{What is deliberately \emph{not} addressed here}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The $L$ degree of freedom.}
        \texttt{Paper2DMMS} hard-codes the spatial shape
        $\sin(\pi x_1)\sin(\pi x_2)$ etc., which is one half-period on the
        unit square but high-frequency garbage on $L \neq 1$. Allowing $L$
        to vary would require generalising the shape to $\sin(\pi x/L)$
        and re-deriving the analytic gradient/Laplacian/forcing terms. The
        present reparametrization keeps $L=1$ and uses only the $U$ degree
        of freedom; the achievable spread reduction is still $\sim 9$
        orders of magnitude on the worst cell.
  \item \textbf{The pressure penalty $\eta = \mathrm{eps\_val}$.}
        The dimensional pressure amplitude $P_c$ varies with the encoding,
        which means $\eta \cdot p^2$ has a different effective weight per
        cell. Empirically the discrete LBB stabilization is the primary
        gauge fix and $\eta$ acts only as a safety net; the rerun will
        validate this in practice. If the sweep reveals an $\eta$-related
        regression, a regime-aware scaling
        $\eta_{\mathrm{cell}} \propto P_c^{-2}$ is a focused follow-up.
  \item \textbf{Solver tolerances.}
        \texttt{ftol}, \texttt{xtol}, \texttt{stagnation\_noise\_floor}
        are absolute. Under the centered encoding the residual magnitudes
        shrink for low-$\Reyn$ cells; the existing
        \texttt{dynamic\_ftol\_spatial\_safety\_factor} machinery scales
        \texttt{ftol} with $h^{k+1}$, which empirically reaches sensible
        values in both encodings (the probes converged to high precision).
        If a regression appears, the dynamic-tolerance formula is the
        first place to look.
\end{itemize}
